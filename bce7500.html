7500 B.C.E.
Burials

One of the earliest pieces of evidence for cat domestication comes from the island of Cyprus, where a cat was found buried alongside a human in a grave dating back to around 7500 BCE.

This discovery suggests that cats were already living in close proximity to humans and may have been considered important companions or even spiritual beings.

The fact that the cat was buried with a human also indicates that the relationship between cats and humans may have been significant enough to indicate domestication.

This finding provides important insights into the early history of the domestication of cats and their evolving relationship with humans.

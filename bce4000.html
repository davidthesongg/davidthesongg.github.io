4000 B.C.E.
Egyptian Art

Cats held a special place in ancient Egyptian culture, being revered for their grace, poise, and hunting skills.

They were often depicted in Egyptian art as symbols of fertility, femininity, and protection, and were associated with various goddesses such as Bastet, the goddess of fertility, pleasure, and childbirth.

In addition to their spiritual significance, cats also played a practical role in ancient Egypt, where they were valued as protectors of grain stores and were kept in homes to control rodents and snakes.

Their importance was reflected in the way they were treated: cats were often mummified and buried with great care, and killing a cat was considered a serious crime punishable by death.

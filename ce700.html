700 C.E.
China

Cats are believed to have been introduced to China around 5,000 years ago.

Initially, they were kept as rodent hunters to protect the crops and grain stores. However, over time, they also became beloved companions and protectors of food stores.

The ancient Chinese recognized the cats' ability to control vermin and appreciated their independent nature.

In fact, cats were often depicted in Chinese art and literature as symbols of good luck and prosperity.

Today, cats are still popular pets in China, and many people consider them as valued members of their families.

1000 C.E.
Viking Traderes in Europe

Cats were brought to Europe by Viking traders around the 8th and 9th centuries.

These traders sailed across the seas with cats on board their ships to control rodents that would eat the precious cargo.

The cats quickly became popular on the continent and were often kept as pets in royal courts. They were especially popular among wealthy women who admired their beauty and grace.

In fact, cats were so highly valued in some European cultures that they were even considered sacred. Today, cats remain one of the most beloved pets in Europe and around the world.

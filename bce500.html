500 B.C.E.
Greece and Rome

Cats were introduced to Greece and Rome through trade and conquest, and quickly became popular as pets due to their ability to control rodents and other pests.

The ancient Greeks and Romans valued cats for their hunting skills, and often kept them in homes, public places, and even in temples.

They believed that cats were sacred animals and associated them with several goddesses, including the Greek goddess Artemis and the Roman goddess Diana.

Over time, cats became an integral part of daily life in these societies, with many people considering them beloved companions.

Today, cats continue to be popular pets in Greece and Rome, and their importance is reflected in the many monuments and artifacts that depict them in art and literature.

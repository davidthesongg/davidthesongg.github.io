1600 C.E.
The Americas

Cats were brought to the Americas by European settlers during the 16th century.

At first, they were mainly kept as pets and for their hunting abilities. However, it didn't take long for people to recognize the value of cats in controlling rodent populations.

Rats and mice had also been introduced to the Americas by European settlers, and they quickly became a major problem, causing damage to crops and spreading disease.

Cats, with their natural instincts as hunters, proved to be excellent at keeping rodent populations in check.

As a result, they became an important part of many households and communities, and their role as rodent controllers helped to keep the spread of disease in check.

Today, cats remain popular pets and are still valued for their hunting abilities and their role in keeping rodent populations under control.

2000 B.C.E.
Companions and Protectors

Cats have been fully domesticated in Egypt for thousands of years and are highly valued as companions and protectors of grain stores.

In modern times, cats are considered an integral part of Egyptian culture, and many households keep them as pets.

Egyptians have a deep love and respect for cats, and it is not uncommon to see them roaming freely in the streets, enjoying the warm sunshine.

Their affectionate nature and ability to protect grain stores from rodents have made them highly valued companions in many households.

It is said that owning a cat in Egypt is not just a privilege, but a cultural legacy that has been passed down through generations.
